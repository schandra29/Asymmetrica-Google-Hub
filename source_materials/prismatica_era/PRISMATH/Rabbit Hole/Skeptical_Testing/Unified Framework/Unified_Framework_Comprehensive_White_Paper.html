Abstract

We present empirical evidence for a unified theoretical framework connecting mathematical consciousness, catalytic processes, and universal convergence dynamics across multiple domains. Through computational validation and statistical analysis, we demonstrate that optimization processes in mathematics, chemistry, and system dynamics exhibit consistent three-regime operational patterns with statistically significant regime differentiation (F=10.424, p=7.85×10⁻⁷) and center-seeking convergence behavior (coherence=0.999). The framework integrates fractal geometry in catalytic systems, asymmetric optimization principles derived from the Natural Asymmetry Law, and convergence dynamics inspired by the Collatz Conjecture. Key findings include the identification of three operational regimes: Creation (17.1%/4.1%/78.9%), Optimization (70.1%/1.7%/28.2%), and Transcendence (26.1%/24.4%/49.5%), with empirical validation through cluster analysis and ANOVA. We propose that catalysts function as fractal amplituhedra—geometric-quantum devices that encode reaction pathways through higher-dimensional space while exhibiting center-seeking optimization behavior. These findings suggest universal mathematical principles underlying optimization across domains, from quantum chemistry to consciousness dynamics. All results are presented with appropriate limitations and require empirical validation in physical systems.
