Level 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

Foundation: Learning to Converse, Not Command

Principle 1: Context Creates Intelligence

‚ùå Wrong: "Write a marketing email for my product"
‚úÖ Right: "I'm working on a marketing email for small businesses in India who struggle with inventory management. They're typically family-run, not very tech-savvy, but eager to grow. Most are using WhatsApp for everything. What angles do you think would resonate? I'm thinking of emphasizing simplicity over features."

Why it works: Context enables AI to consider cultural factors, pain points, emotional triggers, and practical constraints. The response quality improves 5-10x with proper context.

Principle 2: Progressive Context Building

Instead of information dumping, build understanding progressively:

Message 1: "I'm working on business software for Indian SMBs" Message 2: "The main problem they face is inventory tracking" Message 3: "Most still use paper or basic Excel" Message 4: "They're comfortable with WhatsApp but scared of complex software" Message 5: "What if we positioned it as 'WhatsApp-simple inventory'?"

Result: AI builds a mental model progressively, each response getting more targeted and valuable.

Principle 3: The Power of "Stupid" Questions

Ask AI to explain its understanding:

  • "What do you understand about the problem so far?"
  • "What assumptions am I making that might be wrong?"
  • "What am I missing here?"
  • "If you were the customer, what would worry you?"
  • "What would you do differently?"

Breakthrough insight: AI often spots gaps you're blind to because it doesn't share your assumptions.

Level 2: Creative Multiplication (Weeks 3-4)

Creative Multiplication: Unlocking the 10x Zone

The Three-Phase Creative Flow

Phase 1: Wild Exploration (No Limits)

"Let's brainstorm wildly about inventory management. No limits, no constraints. What if the software could predict demand using weather? What if it connected to local WhatsApp groups for crowdsourced data? What if it had voice commands in regional dialects? What if it could run on a 100 rupee feature phone? Just throw crazy ideas at me."

Purpose: Break mental constraints, discover unexpected angles

Phase 2: Strategic Refinement (Making it Real)

"I love the WhatsApp integration idea. Let's refine it. How would that actually work technically? What are the regulatory constraints? What would the user journey look like? What could go wrong? How do we make it foolproof for non-tech users?"

Purpose: Transform wild ideas into practical solutions

Phase 3: Polished Delivery (Ready to Ship)

"Let's create the final version. Simple language that a 50-year-old shop owner would understand. Address their specific fears about technology. Use local examples and metaphors. Make it feel as familiar as WhatsApp but as powerful as enterprise software."

Purpose: Create market-ready output with emotional resonance

The Trust Multiplier Protocol

From Micromanagement to Trust

Instead of: "Write exactly this..." Try: "Here's what I'm trying to achieve: [goal]. Here's the context: [background]. Here's what I've tried: [attempts]. What would you do? Feel free to be creative and challenge my assumptions."
